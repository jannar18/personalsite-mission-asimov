# ASMV-4: Naming Candidates v2

> **Direction:** Two-word names in the register of "Learning to See" -- warm, personal, curious, alive. Not studio names. Not jargon. Names that sound like a person thinking out loud about the world.

## Context

The v1 list was rejected as too cold, too minimal, too "clever studio name." Single Latin/Greek words like Limn, Tekt, Solum read as architectural jargon -- precise but lifeless. The direction now comes from Julianna's original blog name, **"Learning to See,"** which tells us everything about the right register:

- **Present participle** -- ongoing process, not a finished state
- **Sensory/perceptual** -- about vision, awareness, attention
- **Humble** -- admits you don't already know
- **Active** -- you are doing something, not just being something
- **Personal** -- sounds like a person, not a company

Julianna also flagged **"Hidden Dimensions"** as a direction she likes -- two words, evocative, layered meaning.

The name should feel equally at home as the title of a thoughtful personal blog and as a lowercase serif wordmark on an Asimov-quality site. It should carry the warmth of terracotta and cream, not the cold of steel and glass. It should feel like someone who draws trees with more care than buildings, who layers photographs inside line drawings, who believes the ground has as much to say as the architecture.

## Cluster 1: Seeing / Perception

Names about the act of looking, noticing, paying attention -- the "Learning to See" lineage directly.

### 1. Slow Looking

The practice of spending extended time with a single thing -- a drawing, a building, a landscape -- until you see what you missed at first. Art museums use "slow looking" as a pedagogical method. It captures Julianna's approach to architecture (careful observation over quick rendering) and to software (deep understanding over fast shipping). Two words, warm, anti-hustle. Domain: slowlooking.com is likely available; the phrase is known but not branded.

### 2. Seeing Through

Double meaning: seeing through something (transparency, looking past the surface) and seeing something through (persistence, completion). Connects to the layering/transparency in Julianna's collage work -- literally seeing through one layer to another. Also captures the bridge: seeing through the boundary between architecture and software. Domain: seeingthrough.com possible.

### 3. Quiet Eye

A term from sports psychology -- the focused gaze before action. The moment of stillness where perception sharpens. Captures Julianna's restraint (quiet) and her perceptual orientation (eye). Two short words, gentle, a little mysterious. Domain: quieteye.com may be taken due to the sports psychology usage; quieteye.co or quieteye.studio possible.

### 4. Near Sight

Not "nearsight" (the vision condition) but "near sight" -- the act of looking at what's close, paying attention to the immediate. An intimate, present-tense way of seeing. Suggests a person who draws the tree in front of them rather than the skyline in the distance. Domain: nearsight.com possible, though nearness to "nearsighted" may create confusion.

### 5. Looking Closer

Present participle, active, warm. The ongoing practice of paying more attention. Less poetic than some options but extremely clear and human. Someone named their site "Looking Closer" and you'd immediately understand: this is a person who cares about detail, who doesn't skim. Domain: lookingcloser.com possible.

### 6. Soft Focus

A photographic/cinematic technique where sharpness is deliberately reduced -- not blurry, but gently diffuse. Connects to Julianna's watercolor-wash edges, her stipple fields that dissolve into whitespace. The name implies a way of seeing that isn't hard-edged or clinical. Two words, warm, slightly dreamy. Domain: softfocus.com likely taken (photography term); softfocus.studio possible.

## Cluster 2: Making / Becoming

Names about process, growth, craft, building -- the ongoing act of learning through doing.

### 7. Still Building

"Still" does double work: continuing to build (still building, after all this time) and building stillness (quiet, contemplative making). Captures the daily-update energy -- the site is never finished, always in process. Warm, humble, honest. Domain: stillbuilding.com likely available.

### 8. Slow Work

The counterpart to "slow looking." Work that takes the time it needs. Not lazy -- deliberate. The stipple drawing that takes a thousand tiny marks. The codebase built with care. Connects to the slow food / slow design movement without being preachy. Domain: slowwork.com may be taken; slowwork.studio possible.

### 9. Gathering Lines

The act of collecting -- lines of thought, lines of drawing, lines of code. "Gathering" is warm and physical (gathering flowers, gathering materials). "Lines" bridges architecture (drawing lines) and software (lines of code) without being literal about either. Domain: gatheringlines.com likely available.

### 10. Making Room

Double meaning: creating space (architectural, literal) and making room for something new (philosophical, metaphorical). The act of clearing space so something can emerge. Warm, domestic, generous. Domain: makingroom.com likely taken; makingroom.studio possible.

### 11. Rough Draft

Honest, unpretentious. The thing before the thing. A site called "Rough Draft" promises process over polish -- which is interesting tension with the Asimov-quality execution. The name would be humble; the site would be beautiful. That gap is charming. Domain: roughdraft.com certainly taken; roughdraft.studio possible. Collision: Rough Draft Ventures exists.

### 12. Daily Practice

What the site literally is -- a daily practice made visible. Carries the discipline of an architect's morning sketches and the regularity of a software engineer's commits. Simple, warm, earnest. Domain: dailypractice.com likely taken; dailypractice.studio possible. May feel too generic.

## Cluster 3: Light / Revelation

Names about illumination, uncovering, making the invisible visible.

### 13. Slow Light

Light that takes its time -- the way morning light moves across a room over hours, the way understanding arrives gradually. Poetic, warm, slightly scientific (slow light is an actual physics phenomenon -- light passing through certain media at dramatically reduced speeds). Domain: slowlight.com may be available; slowlight.studio promising.

### 14. First Light

The earliest light of day. The moment before dawn when things become visible. Carries beginnings, hope, perception -- you're seeing something for the first time. Domain: firstlight.com certainly taken; firstlight.studio possible. The phrase is common but beautiful.

### 15. Warm Signal

A signal that carries warmth -- not the cold precision of a digital signal but something human transmitted through noise. Bridges the technical (signal processing, software) and the sensory (warmth, touch). Domain: warmsignal.com likely available. Unusual enough to own.

### 16. Thin Light

Architectural term: the quality of light that enters through a narrow opening -- a clerestory, a slit window, a gap between buildings. Thin light is focused, directional, revealing. It draws attention to specific things. Domain: thinlight.com likely available.

### 17. Afterglow

The light that remains after the source is gone -- sunset afterglow, the persistence of vision. Warm, sensory, slightly melancholic. Suggests something that lingers and keeps giving. Domain: afterglow.com certainly taken; afterglow.studio possible. Common word but beautiful.

## Cluster 4: Space / Dimension

Names about hidden layers, depth, the unseen -- the "Hidden Dimensions" lineage.

### 18. Hidden Dimensions

Julianna's own suggestion. Two words, evocative, layered meaning. The dimensions you don't see at first -- the software thinking inside the architecture, the spatial thinking inside the code. Also a literal reference to theoretical physics (extra spatial dimensions) and to architectural drawing (the dimensions hidden in a plan that a section reveals). Domain: hiddendimensions.com may be available; long but memorable.

### 19. Deeper Ground

The earth beneath the earth. What you find when you dig. Connects to Julianna's landscape consciousness (the ground matters) and to the act of going deeper into understanding. Warm, grounded, suggests excavation and discovery. Domain: deeperground.com possible; connects to the earth palette.

### 20. Inner Terrain

The landscape inside -- mental, emotional, intellectual terrain. Bridges the literal landscape of Julianna's architecture work with the inner exploration of "Learning to See." Domain: innerterrain.com likely available. Note: "Terrain" is an Asimov client -- but "Inner Terrain" is sufficiently distinct.

### 21. Visible Layers

What you see when you look carefully -- the layers beneath the surface. Connects directly to the collage/layering design language (seeing through transparent layers) and to the discipline bridge (layers of meaning across architecture and software). Domain: visiblelayers.com likely available.

### 22. Other Rooms

The rooms you haven't entered yet. The spaces adjacent to where you are. Architectural but not jargon -- everyone knows what a room is. Suggests curiosity, exploration, the feeling that there's always more to discover. Domain: otherrooms.com possible.

### 33. Quiet Study

### 34. Patient Eye

An eye that waits. That doesn't rush to judgment. That lets the thing reveal itself over time. Connected to "slow looking" but with more warmth -- patience implies care. Domain: patienteye.com likely available.

An unusual construction -- "wide" modifying "curious." Wide-eyed and curious. Suggests breadth of interest (architecture AND software AND AI AND landscape) combined with genuine curiosity. Domain: widecurious.com likely available. Distinctive.

### 37. Turning Over

The act of turning something over in your hands or mind -- examining it from all sides. Also: turning over soil (planting, groundwork) and turning over a new page (beginning). Rich with layered meaning. Domain: turningover.com possible.

## Notes on Domain Strategy

For any two-word name, the primary domain target should be `[wordword].com` (no hyphen). If unavailable:
- `.studio` is the strongest alternative for a creative practice
- `.co` reads well for short names
- Hyphenated `.com` (e.g., `slow-looking.com`) is acceptable but less clean
- Avoid `.io` for this project -- it reads as purely tech, which misses the architecture/warmth side

Two-word names are inherently longer as domains but more memorable and searchable. The tradeoff is worth it for the warmth and humanity they carry.

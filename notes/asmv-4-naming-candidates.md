# ASMV-4: Naming Candidates

## Context

Julianna is a practitioner across two disciplines -- architecture (academic/studio work: renderings, drawings, conceptual projects with deep landscape consciousness) and software engineering / AI research -- who needs a unified identity artifact. The name must capture this intersection without being literal about any single domain. It should carry the restraint, warmth, and intellectual seriousness of the Asimov Collective quality bar while reflecting Julianna's specific sensibility: warm earth tones, stipple and grain textures, watercolor edges, collage as thinking method, quiet European rigor, and the conviction that the hand and the machine are not opposed. The site IS the bridge between architecture and software -- the name should feel equally at home on an architectural drawing sheet and a GitHub profile.

## Naming Criteria

- Works as a `.com` or compelling TLD (`.studio`, `.works`, `.design`, `.io`, `.co`)
- Looks good set in type -- particularly in a serif, light-weight, lowercase wordmark
- Does not collide with established brands in architecture, tech, or design
- Feels appropriate at the quality level of an Asimov Collective site
- Can scale: personal site today, practice or studio later
- Pronounceable, memorable, not easily misspelled
- Short enough to work as a wordmark (1-3 syllables ideal, 4 acceptable)

## Candidates by Family

### Family A: Spatial / Architectural Metaphor

Names drawn from architectural vocabulary that carry resonance in software, systems, or philosophical contexts.

1. **Datum** -- In architecture, a datum is a fixed reference line, plane, or point from which measurements are taken. In computing, a datum is a single piece of information. Both disciplines share the word to mean "the thing you orient from." Short, crisp, Latin. Looks beautiful in type. Domain concern: datum.com is taken, but datum.studio, datum.works, datum.io likely available.

2. **Parti** -- The fundamental design concept or scheme of a building. In French, literally "decision taken." It's the irreducible diagram of an idea. In software, a parti is the core architectural decision that everything else follows from. Short, elegant, French. Slightly insider (architects know it instantly; others will learn it). parti.com likely taken; parti.studio is promising.

3. **Threshold** -- The boundary between inside and outside, between one condition and another. In software: threshold values, trigger points, state transitions. As a personal brand, it captures the liminal position of someone between disciplines. Two syllables, strong in English. threshold.studio or threshold.works would work well. threshold.com is certainly taken.

4. **Section** -- An architectural drawing that cuts through a building to reveal its interior relationships. In software: a section of code, a division of content. The word implies revelation -- cutting through to see what's inside. section.studio is promising. Clean, single word, universally understood.

5. **Foyer** -- The entrance space where transition happens. Not quite inside, not quite outside. In French, literally "hearth" or "home." A foyer is where you arrive and orient. As a site name, it positions the site as an arrival point, a threshold into Julianna's world. foyer.studio, foyer.co both promising.

6. **Soffit** -- The underside of an architectural element -- what you see when you look up beneath an overhang, arch, or staircase. Technically precise, aesthetically specific: soffits are where light and shadow create atmosphere. Unusual enough to be ownable. soffit.studio, soffit.co likely available.

7. **Lintel** -- The horizontal element that spans an opening and carries the load above. Structurally essential but often invisible. A bridge between two supports -- a direct metaphor for bridging disciplines. lintel.studio, lintel.co, lintel.dev all promising.

8. **Plinth** -- The base on which something rests. In architecture, the foundation course. In display, the platform for presenting work. Suggests groundedness and the act of elevating. Short, uncommon enough to own. plinth.studio, plinth.co likely available.

9. **Clerestory** -- The upper section of a wall with windows that admit light from above. A beautiful word that implies illumination coming from unexpected heights. Four syllables may be long for a wordmark, but pronounced "CLEAR-story" it has a lovely ring and an accidental meaning (clear story). clerestory.studio possible.

10. **Aperture** -- An opening that admits light. In architecture: windows, doors, any penetration of a wall plane. In photography and optics: the opening that controls how much light enters. Suggests openness, perception, letting light in. aperture.studio -- but Aperture is already used by Apple (defunct photo app) and Valve (Portal's fictional lab). Collision risk is moderate.

### Family B: Process / Making Metaphor

Names that emphasize the act of making, building, drafting, or synthesizing across disciplines.

11. **Trace** -- In architecture: tracing paper, the fundamental medium for iterating on ideas. In software: execution tracing, stack traces, debugging. In drawing: the act of following a line. The word carries process, investigation, and the hand. trace.studio, trace.works possible. trace.com is taken.

12. **Draft** -- An architectural drawing. A rough version. A current of air. Each meaning implies something in progress, not finished -- which aligns with the "living site" concept. One syllable, strong. draft.studio, draft.works possible. draft.com taken.

13. **Proof** -- A test print. A demonstration of truth. A stage in the making process between draft and final. In typography, a proof is a trial impression. In logic, a proof is a complete argument. Bridges making and rigor. proof.studio, proof.works possible.

14. **Assemblage** -- A collection of found objects and materials composed into a work. In architecture and art: the practice of gathering and composing disparate elements into a coherent whole. This IS what Julianna does -- assembling architecture, software, and AI into a unified practice. Four syllables is long but the word is distinctive. assemblage.studio possible.

15. **Imprint** -- The mark left by pressing one thing into another. In publishing: a publisher's label. In making: the physical evidence of process. Carries both the hand (pressing) and the intellectual (publishing). imprint.studio possible but imprint has publishing connotations that may dominate.

16. **Graft** -- To join a shoot or bud to a rootstock so they grow as one. The biological metaphor for combining two living practices into a unified organism. Also: to work hard (British usage). graft.studio, graft.works possible. Distinctive, short, vivid.

17. **Transfer** -- The act of moving something from one surface or context to another. In Julianna's own work: transfer prints, monoprints -- the process of pressing one material into another. In computing: data transfer. In architecture: load transfer. The word is about the movement between disciplines. transfer.studio possible.

18. **Studio Rubbing** -- A rubbing is a technique of transferring texture by placing paper over a surface and drawing across it. From Julianna's actual sketchbook practice. As a two-word name it's long, but "Rubbing" alone is evocative: the physical act of revealing what's beneath the surface. rubbing.studio possible, though the word carries unfortunate alternate connotations in some contexts.

19. **Stipple** -- The technique of creating texture through thousands of tiny marks. This is the single strongest drawing technique across all of Julianna's references. As a name, it's precise, tactile, and immediately evocative of process. stipple.studio, stipple.co possible.

20. **Verso** -- The left-hand page of an open book (opposite of recto). In printing and bookbinding: the reverse side. Implies publication, careful making, and duality (there's always another side). Short, Italian/Latin, typographically elegant. verso.studio, verso.co possible. Some collision: Verso Books is an established publisher.

### Family C: Natural / Landscape

Names drawn from the landscape consciousness that runs through Julianna's work -- topography, ground, water systems, vegetation, the relationship between built form and earth.

21. **Meridian** -- An imaginary line of longitude running from pole to pole. In anatomy, energy pathways. Suggests orientation, navigation, a line that connects. meridian.studio possible. The word is used by many brands but rarely in architecture/tech intersection.

22. **Contour** -- A line connecting points of equal elevation. The fundamental unit of topographic mapping. In design: the outline shape of something. Julianna's work shows a deep contour/topographic obsession. contour.studio, contour.co possible.

23. **Understory** -- The layer of vegetation beneath the forest canopy. Quiet, shaded, essential to the ecosystem but not immediately visible. As a metaphor: the work happening beneath the surface, the foundation that supports what's visible. understory.studio, understory.co likely available.

24. **Riparian** -- Relating to the banks of a river or stream. Julianna's portfolio shows river corridors, watershed thinking, water systems as organizing principles. A beautiful, uncommon word. riparian.studio likely available. May be difficult to spell for some.

25. **Terrane** -- A geological term: a fragment of crustal material formed on one tectonic plate and attached to another. The geological version of "graft" -- a piece of one world that becomes part of another. Visually close to "terrain" (which Asimov used for a client) but etymologically distinct. terrane.studio, terrane.co possible.

26. **Canopy** -- The uppermost layer of a forest, where trees reach light. In architecture: an overhanging roof or shelter. In Julianna's work, trees and their canopies are drawn with more care than buildings. canopy.studio is appealing, but Canopy is used by several companies (Amazon's Canopy, etc.).

27. **Estuary** -- Where a river meets the sea -- a zone of mixing, transition, and extraordinary productivity. The ecological metaphor for the intersection of disciplines. Three syllables, flowing sound. estuary.studio, estuary.co possible.

28. **Watershed** -- The boundary line between drainage basins. Also: a turning point, a moment of fundamental change. Carries both the landscape meaning and the philosophical meaning. watershed.studio possible but the word is commonly used.

29. **Moraine** -- The accumulation of earth and stones carried and deposited by a glacier. Geological, slow, powerful. The idea of things being gathered and deposited over time by a force that's patient and enormous. moraine.studio, moraine.co likely available.

30. **Talus** -- The sloped mass of rock fragments at the base of a cliff. In anatomy: the ankle bone. In architecture: the sloped base of a fortification wall. A place where things accumulate at the edge of something larger. Short, unusual, elegant. talus.studio, talus.co likely available.

### Family D: Conceptual / Abstract

Invented words, portmanteaus, or borrowed words from other languages that carry the right feeling without literal English meaning.

31. **Arke** -- From the Greek "arkhe" (beginning, origin, first cause) and a visual echo of "arc" and "architecture." Pronounced "ark." Three letters, one syllable. Implies origin, structure, the first principle. arke.studio, arke.co possible. The ".com" may be taken but .studio is promising.

32. **Limn** -- An English word meaning "to draw or paint, especially in outline." From the Latin "illuminare." One syllable, four letters, beautiful in type. Implies the act of drawing, of making visible. limn.studio, limn.co possible. Uncommon enough to be ownable.

33. **Tekt** -- From the Greek "tekton" (builder, carpenter) -- the root of both "architect" and "technology." Four letters that literally contain the shared root of Julianna's two disciplines. tekt.studio, tekt.co, tekt.io all likely available.

34. **Sgraffito** -- An Italian technique of scratching through a surface layer to reveal a different color beneath. Used in both architecture (facade decoration) and art (ceramics, printmaking). The act of revealing by removing. sgraffito.studio possible. Four syllables but rhythmically satisfying. May be difficult to spell.

35. **Stoa** -- An ancient Greek covered walkway or colonnade, open on one side. The Stoic philosophers taught in the Stoa Poikile. A space for walking, thinking, and being in public. Four letters, two syllables, ancient. stoa.studio, stoa.co possible.

36. **Kairos** -- Ancient Greek: the right, critical, or opportune moment. Distinguished from "chronos" (sequential time). Implies timeliness, the Now page, the daily update as capturing the moment. kairos.studio possible. Some brand collision (Kairos is used by several companies).

37. **Opus** -- Latin for "work," as in a body of work, a composition, a life's output. "Magnum opus" = great work. Short, direct, carries gravitas. opus.studio possible. Collision note: several companies use Opus, including Anthropic's Claude Opus model.

38. **Topos** -- Greek for "place." The root of "topography," "topology," "utopia" (no-place). In mathematics: a category that behaves like the category of sets. In rhetoric: a commonplace, a conventional motif. Bridges architecture (place-making) and computation (topology, mathematical space). topos.studio, topos.co possible.

39. **Tessera** -- A small block of stone, glass, or tile used in making a mosaic. Latin. The idea of small, precise pieces assembled into a larger picture. Relates to the collage/assemblage quality in Julianna's work. tessera.studio, tessera.co possible.

40. **Atelier Terre** -- French: "earth workshop" or "workshop of the ground." Combines the studio/atelier tradition with the warm-earth sensibility of Julianna's palette and landscape consciousness. Two words, five syllables total. As a domain: atelierterre.com, atelierterre.studio possible. Carries a distinctly European register.

41. **Palimpsest** -- A manuscript page that has been written on, scraped off, and written on again, with traces of earlier text visible beneath. The metaphor for layered thinking, accumulated work, traces of process. Architecturally rich (buildings as palimpsests of habitation). Four syllables, hard to spell. palimpsest.studio possible.

42. **Velum** -- Latin for "veil" or "covering." In architecture: a vault or canopy. In botany: a membrane. In drafting: vellum is the translucent paper used for architectural drawings. Spelled "velum" (one L) it's distinct from the drafting paper while evoking it. velum.studio, velum.co possible.

43. **Solum** -- Latin for "ground, soil, foundation." The basis on which everything rests. In soil science: the upper layer of earth. Connects to Julianna's landscape consciousness and groundedness. Short, Latin, typographically clean. solum.studio, solum.co likely available.

### Family E: First-Person / Eponymous

Using Julianna's own name, possibly with a qualifier. The site is a personal identity artifact -- an eponymous name is honest and carries the "unified practice" message inherently.

*Note: These candidates use "Julianna" as provided in project documents. Surname-based variants would be developed once Julianna confirms which form she prefers.*

44. **Julianna** -- The name alone, as the domain and identity. Simple, personal, unmistakable. Works if the chosen TLD is distinctive: julianna.studio, julianna.design, julianna.works. The name itself is strong -- four syllables with a musical quality. A serif lowercase "julianna" as wordmark would be beautiful.

45. **J. Studio** -- Initial + qualifier. Minimal, elegant, in the tradition of architectural practices that abbreviate to initials. Depends on surname initial for distinctiveness. jstudio.co, jstudio.design possible.

46. **Studio Julianna** -- "Studio" as prefix, in the European tradition (Studio Mumbai, Studio Olafur Eliasson). Positions the site as a practice from day one. studiojulianna.com possible.

47. **Julianna Works** -- "Works" carries both meanings: a body of work (portfolio) and the act of working (process). juliannaworks.com possible. Scales naturally -- "Julianna Works" as a practice name has legs.

48. **Casa Julianna** -- Italian/Spanish for "house." In the tradition of fashion and design houses (Casa Loewe, Casa Vicens). Warm, architectural, personal. casajulianna.com possible.

## Top 10 Recommendations

These are personal recommendations based on how well each name serves the intersection of architecture + software + AI, how it looks in type, how it sounds, and how it would feel on an Asimov-quality site. Julianna makes the final choice.

### 1. Limn
Family D (Conceptual/Abstract). One syllable, four letters. Means "to draw or paint, especially in outline." From Latin "illuminare." This is the strongest single candidate: it's short enough for a powerful wordmark, it's about the act of making visible, it bridges drawing and revelation, and it's uncommon enough that limn.studio or limn.co is likely available. Set in a light-weight serif lowercase, "limn" is visually striking. It sounds like the beginning of "liminal."

### 2. Tekt
Family D (Conceptual/Abstract). One syllable, four letters. From Greek "tekton" (builder) -- the shared etymological root of "architect" and "technology." This is the most conceptually precise choice: the name literally encodes the bridge between Julianna's two disciplines. tekt.studio, tekt.io available. Short, modern, distinctive.

### 3. Datum
Family A (Spatial/Architectural). Two syllables. A fixed reference point in both architecture and computing -- the thing you orient everything else from. Universally understood across disciplines. datum.studio works. Clean, Latin, carries authority.

### 4. Contour
Family C (Natural/Landscape). Two syllables. The fundamental line of topographic mapping, connecting points of equal elevation. Reflects Julianna's deep landscape consciousness. contour.studio is elegant. The word is known but not overused as a brand.

### 5. Trace
Family B (Process/Making). One syllable. Tracing paper in architecture, execution tracing in software, the act of following a line. Carries process, investigation, and the hand. trace.studio works. Simple, evocative, scales well.

### 6. Solum
Family D (Conceptual/Abstract). Two syllables. Latin for "ground, soil, foundation." Connects to landscape consciousness, groundedness, and the idea of building on solid earth. solum.studio, solum.co likely available. Typographically beautiful -- the round letters (s, o, u) give it warmth.

### 7. Stoa
Family D (Conceptual/Abstract). Two syllables. The ancient Greek covered walkway where Stoic philosophers taught. A space for walking, thinking, and being in public. stoa.studio is appealing. Four letters, ancient resonance, implies both structure and thought.

### 8. Parti
Family A (Spatial/Architectural). Two syllables. The fundamental design concept, the irreducible diagram of an idea. Instantly recognizable to architects, learnable by others. parti.studio works. French elegance in type.

### 9. Graft
Family B (Process/Making). One syllable. The biological act of joining two living things so they grow as one. The most vivid metaphor for combining architecture and software into a unified practice. graft.studio, graft.works available. Short, physical, memorable.

### 10. Topos
Family D (Conceptual/Abstract). Two syllables. Greek for "place" -- the root of topography, topology, utopia. Bridges architecture (place-making) and computation (mathematical topology). topos.studio works. Five letters, classical, carries intellectual weight without pretension.

### My Notes

#### Section 1: Elementary Rules of Usage

- Rules 3, 4, 5 and 6 cover the most important principles that govern punctuation: enclose parenthetic expressions between commas, place a comma before a conjunction introducing an independent clause, do not join independent clauses with a comma, do not break sentences in two.
- Why are 3, 4, 5 + 6 the most important principles?
- What is the grammatical subject vs. the subject?
- In order to understand whether a sentence is right or wrong, it is crucial to break sentences down into their parts. I have a weak habit of practicing this.
- What does Elementary Rules of Usage mean?

#### Section 2: Elementary Principles of Composition

- Effective writing calls for a scheme of procedure; the first principle of composition is to determine the shape of what is to come and pursue that shape.
- The paragraph is a convenient unit and a good visual help.
- Use the active voice.
- Use the positive form. "Not" is weak. The reader wishes to be told what is.
- Be assertive; save the auxiliaries.
- Use definite, specific, concrete language: "He grinned as he pocketed the coin."
- Use specific language; call up pictures with your words.
- Omit "the fact that," "who is," and "which was" from your writing vocabulary.
- Keep related words together. "There was a stir in the audience that suggested disapproval" vs. "A stir that suggested disapproval swept the audience." "She only found two mistakes" vs. "She found only two mistakes."
- After the initial notification, waste no words in repeating the notification of an author/speaker.
- Place what is to be made most prominent, word, sentence or paragraph, at the end.

#### Section 3: A Few Matters of Form

- Quotations are more complex than I've practiced in my writing.

#### Section 4: Words and Expressions Commonly Misused

- There are many words and phrases to eliminate from my use: and/or, anticipate, case, character, currently, each and every one, facility, factor, feature, "he is a man who," importantly, in terms of, meaningful, "one of the most," personally, respective, respectively, secondly, thirdly etc., "the truth is," utilize, while.
- "Get" is complex. Be careful of "would."

A real-time party game built around Thomas Schelling's focal point theory — the idea that people can coordinate without communication by gravitating toward answers that feel obvious. Players join a lobby, then face 20 rounds of single-word prompts. Everyone answers independently. The game scores how well the group converges.

The interface uses an orbital visualization — colored dots representing players drift around concentric rings. When answers align, the dots collapse inward. A "Mind Meld" triggers confetti. The whole thing runs over WebSockets with a Node relay server, React Native on the client side, and Expo for cross-platform builds.
